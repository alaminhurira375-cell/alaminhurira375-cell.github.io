Teaching Philosophy

I view economics teaching as training in disciplined inquiry. Students should learn not only formal models and empirical tools, but also how to ask precise questions, evaluate evidence, and communicate findings with intellectual honesty. My courses aim to build analytical confidence through structured problem solving, transparent assessment, and policy applications that make abstract concepts concrete.

In applied courses, I emphasize reproducible empirical work, interpretation over mechanical calculation, and the limits of causal claims. I work to create a classroom environment in which students from varied backgrounds can engage seriously with economic ideas and leave with skills that transfer to research, policy, and professional practice.
