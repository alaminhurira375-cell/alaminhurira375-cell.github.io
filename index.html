About

Welcome to my website. I am a PhD student in Agricultural and Applied Economics at the University of Georgia.

My research focuses on food policy, nutrition assistance programs, and public health economics, with particular emphasis on food security, diet quality, and household well-being.

My dissertation, Nutrition Assistance Programs, Food Security, and Diet Quality in the United States , studies participation and benefit redemption in the Special Supplemental Nutrition Program for Women, Infants, and Children (WIC), school meal policy, and the measurement of food hardship.

My broader research interests include applied microeconomics, nutrition economics, development economics, and environmental economics.

Advisor: Travis Alan Smith, Associate Professor, Department of Agricultural and Applied Economics, University of Georgia.

Selected Research

The Effects of WIC on Household Food Security and the Quality of Food Acquisitions

Dissertation Chapter 1 / Job Market Paper

This paper examines whether participation in WIC improves household food security and the nutritional quality of food-at-home acquisitions. The analysis uses USDA FoodAPS data and compares WIC participants with eligible nonparticipants while distinguishing between households that redeemed their benefits and those that did not.

Read more

How Would Changes to Community Eligibility Provision Thresholds Affect Child Diet Quality?

Dissertation Chapter 2 / AAEA 2026 Conference Paper

This project examines how changes in school meal eligibility rules may affect child diet quality by combining school-level Community Eligibility Provision data with dietary intake evidence from NHANES.

Read more

Measuring the Distance to Food Sufficiency: A Continuous Approach to Food Hardship

Dissertation Chapter 3 / SEA 2026 Conference Paper

This paper applies a continuous needs-based approach to measure the depth and distribution of food hardship in the United States using data from the Current Population Survey Food Security Supplement.
