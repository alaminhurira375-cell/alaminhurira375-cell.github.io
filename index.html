PhD Student, Agricultural and Applied Economics

Md Al Amin

I am a doctoral student at the University of Georgia whose research examines how public policy, environmental change, energy systems, and development processes shape health and economic well-being.

  • Public Health Economics
  • Environmental Economics
  • Development Economics
  • Energy Economics

My work is empirical and policy-oriented, combining applied microeconometric methods with administrative, survey, environmental, and geospatial data. I am especially interested in questions that connect local policy environments to household welfare, health outcomes, and long-run development.
