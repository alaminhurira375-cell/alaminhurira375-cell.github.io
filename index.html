About

Welcome to my website. I am a PhD student in Agricultural and Applied Economics at the University of Georgia. My research focuses on food policy, nutrition assistance programs, and public health economics, with particular emphasis on food security, diet quality, and household well-being. My dissertation, Nutrition Assistance Programs, Food Security, and Diet Quality in the United States, studies participation and benefit redemption in the Special Supplemental Nutrition Program for Women, Infants, and Children (WIC), school meal policy, and the measurement of food hardship. My broader research interests include applied microeconomics, nutrition economics, development economics, and environmental economics.

Advisor: Travis Alan Smith, Associate Professor, Department of Agricultural and Applied Economics, University of Georgia.
