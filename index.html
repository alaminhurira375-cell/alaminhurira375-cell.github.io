I am a PhD student in Agricultural and Applied Economics at the University of Georgia. My research focuses on food policy, nutrition assistance programs, and public health economics, with particular emphasis on food security, diet quality, and household well-being.

  • Applied Microeconomics

My dissertation, Nutrition Assistance Programs, Food Security, and Diet Quality in the United States, studies WIC, school meal policy, and food hardship measurement using household survey data, dietary intake data, and applied econometric methods.

Advisor: Travis Alan Smith, Associate Professor, Department of Agricultural and Applied Economics, University of Georgia.

Featured Research

The Effects of WIC on Household Food Security and the Quality of Food Acquisitions

Job market paper / Dissertation Chapter 1

This paper studies whether participation in the Special Supplemental Nutrition Program for Women, Infants, and Children improves household food security and the nutritional quality of food-at-home acquisitions. The analysis uses USDA FoodAPS data and propensity score matching to compare WIC participants with eligible nonparticipants.

Read more

How Would Changes to Community Eligibility Provision Thresholds Affect Child Diet Quality?

Working paper / AAEA 2026 accepted conference paper

This project examines how changes in school meal eligibility rules may affect child diet quality by linking school-level CEP policy variation with dietary intake evidence from NHANES.
