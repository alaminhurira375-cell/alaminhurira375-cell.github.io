Does the Informal Economy Undermine Political Stability? Empirical Evidence from Global Perspectives

Economics of Governance [Springer], 27, Article 32 (2026).

Abstract

Political stability (PS) is a cornerstone of economic development, yet the role of the informal economy (IE) in shaping it remains underexplored. This study addresses this gap by examining how informal and formal economies influence PS across 124 countries from 1996 to 2020, with particular attention to income-group heterogeneity. Using the method of moments quantile regression (MMQR) alongside advanced econometric techniques, the study finds that IE systematically undermines PS, while formal-sector macroeconomic performance (FMP) enhances PS. These effects vary meaningfully in magnitude and direction across the PS distribution and income groups. Regulatory quality (RQ), as a moderating factor, significantly mitigates the negative consequences of IE on PS. The study further identifies heterogeneous effects of IE on PS across different country groups. In contrast, FMP and RQ substantially contribute to improving PS in the global context, although their effects also vary across country groups. These findings contribute a novel distributional perspective to the literature on economic structure and governance and carry direct policy implications: reducing informal activity and strengthening institutional frameworks are context-dependent strategies whose effectiveness depends critically on a country's development stage and baseline stability level.

The Role of Fintech and the Shadow Economy in Advancing Sustainable Development Across G7 Countries

Sustainable Development [Wiley].

Abstract

Achieving sustainable development requires balancing economic growth with environmental conservation, but the relationship between informal economic activities and emerging financial technologies in boosting green growth (GG) is not well understood. The current study identifies this gap by considering the impact of the shadow economy (SE) and financial technology, or fintech (FT), on GG in G7 countries from 1996 to 2020. In contrast to previous studies in which these factors are considered separately, the research presents a comprehensive approach that simultaneously investigates the effects of SE and FT across the conditional distribution of GG. The study uses method of moments quantile regression (MMQR), with fully modified ordinary least squares, dynamic ordinary least squares, and canonical cointegration regression to confirm robustness. The results indicate that SE has a statistically significant negative impact on FT and GG. Conversely, FT makes a positive and significant contribution to GG, underscoring its potential role in promoting environmental sustainability.

Relationship between Body Mass Index, Eating Disorders, Internet Addiction, and Social Media Addictions among Adolescents in Bangladesh

Public Health Challenges [Wiley].

Abstract

Background. Due to the increasing consumption of the internet, several outcomes are often seen among adolescents, including an increase in internet addiction and social media addiction. These outcomes may also be associated with body mass index and eating disorders. This study aimed to assess the relationships among BMI, eating disorders, internet addiction, and social media addiction among adolescents in Bangladesh.

Methodology. Using a cross-sectional design, this study gathered data from adolescents aged 13–19 years using an online questionnaire consisting of sociodemographic variables, BMI, the Eating Attitudes Test, Young's Internet Addiction Test, and the Bergen Social Media Addiction Scale.

Results. A total of 2,147 adolescents with a mean age of 15.6 years participated in the study, of whom 70.7% were female. We found that 23.2% of participants had eating disorders, 30.8% were addicted to the internet, and 59.9% were addicted to social media. Internet addiction, social media addiction, and eating disorders were positively correlated.

Conclusion. The findings suggest that policymakers, educators, and parents should encourage adolescents to engage in physical exercise and reading habits to reduce internet and social media addiction. Greater parental awareness regarding BMI and eating disorders is also important for improving adolescent health outcomes.

Protected Vegetable Crop Production for Long-Term Sustainable Food Security

Journal of Scientific Research and Reports, 30(5), 660–669 (2024).

Abstract

A protected culture strategy that offers a fully regulated environment decreases a number of biotic and abiotic stressors. Thinking about protected farming is necessary given the rising food need. The most effective way of meeting the goals of protected horticulture is the greenhouse. These technologies create opportunities for producers with both larger and smaller landholdings. Protected agriculture provides a way to produce more in a small space in response to landholding restrictions, rapid urbanization, declining crop yields, biodiversity loss, and population growth.

Beachgoers' Knowledge, Perceptions, and Willingness to Pay for Sustainable Waste Management in Kuakata Sea Beach, Bangladesh

Nature Environment & Pollution Technology, 23(2) (2024).

Abstract

With rising public awareness and concern for environmental sustainability, calls for environmentally friendly marine and beach litter management have grown louder. Using data collected from 400 visitors to Kuakata Sea Beach in Bangladesh, this study applies logistic and ordinary least squares regressions to examine tourists’ knowledge, perceptions, and willingness to pay for improved waste management. The results show strong public support for further development of the waste-management system. Gender and income are identified as significant factors associated with willingness to pay.

Perilous Resurgence of Dengue Fever in Bangladesh: Gender-Based Perspectives on Risk Perception and Adaptation Strategies

Universal Journal of Public Health, 11(5), 751–760 (2023).

Abstract

Despite achieving substantial success in controlling infectious diseases, Bangladesh has experienced an unprecedented resurgence of dengue fever. This paper examines whether men and women perceive dengue-related risks differently and whether they respond differently through preventive behavior. Using cross-sectional survey data collected from urban areas of the Barishal division, the study applies sociodemographic analysis and logistic regression models. The findings indicate that overall risk perception is low, but female respondents perceive dengue as a more serious threat and are more proactive in adopting preventive measures.

DOI
Md. Tanvir Ahmed and Md. Al Amin. 2023. “Perilous Resurgence of Dengue Fever in Bangladesh: Gender-Based Perspectives on Risk Perception and Adaptation Strategies.” Universal Journal of Public Health, 11(5), 751–760.
