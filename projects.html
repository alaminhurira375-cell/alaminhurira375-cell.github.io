Dissertation Research Pipeline

Chapter 1: The Effects of WIC on Household Food Security and the Quality of Food Acquisitions

Research Question: Does participation in the Special Supplemental Nutrition Program for Women, Infants, and Children (WIC) improve household food security and the nutritional quality of food acquisitions?

Data: USDA FoodAPS (2012–2013)

Identification Strategy: Propensity Score Matching (PSM) to address selection into program participation.

Status: Dissertation Chapter 1 / Job Market Paper

Chapter 2: How Would Changes to Community Eligibility Provision Thresholds Affect Child Diet Quality?

Research Question: How would changes in CEP eligibility rules affect school meal access and child diet quality?

Data: CEP Administrative Data, FRAC, NCES, NHANES (2013–2023)

Identification Strategy: Fixed-effects estimation linking school meal consumption to Healthy Eating Index scores and policy simulations.

Status: AAEA 2026 Accepted Conference Paper

Chapter 3: Measuring the Distance to Food Sufficiency: A Continuous Approach to Food Hardship

Research Question: Can food hardship be measured more accurately using a continuous resource-gap approach rather than binary food insecurity categories?

Data: Current Population Survey Food Security Supplement (2002–2023)

Identification Strategy: Construction of a normalized needs index using household expenditure shortfalls.

Status: Dissertation Chapter 3
