Current Projects

Research Projects

These projects represent the core of my current research agenda and are intended to develop into publishable papers and policy-relevant evidence.

Environmental Exposure and Health Investment

Research question: How do households adjust health investments and economic behavior when environmental conditions deteriorate?

Data sources: Environmental monitoring data, geospatial measures, household surveys, and administrative socioeconomic indicators.

Methodology: Quasi-experimental research design, panel data methods, and heterogeneity analysis.

Current status: Working paper in preparation.

Energy Access and Development Outcomes

Research question: What are the welfare and productivity effects of improved energy access in developing regions?

Data sources: Household survey data, infrastructure records, satellite-derived measures, and local economic indicators.

Methodology: Difference-in-differences, event-study designs, and distributional analysis.

Current status: Data construction and empirical design refinement.

Public Health Policy and Rural Economic Resilience

Research question: How do public health policies affect labor supply, household welfare, and local resilience in rural economies?

Data sources: Policy variation, public health indicators, labor market data, and county-level socioeconomic measures.

Methodology: Applied microeconometric evaluation with robustness checks and subgroup analysis.

Current status: Work in progress.
