Research

Food Policy, Nutrition Assistance, and Applied Microeconomics

My research examines how public policies and economic conditions shape food security, diet quality, public health, and household well-being. My dissertation focuses on nutrition assistance programs in the United States, including WIC, school meal policy, and food hardship measurement.

Job Market Paper

The Effects of WIC on Household Food Security and the Quality of Food Acquisitions

Md Al Amin and Travis Alan Smith. Dissertation Chapter 1 / Job Market Paper.

Abstract

We examine the effect of participation in the Special Supplemental Nutrition Program for WIC on household food security and the quality of food acquisitions using data from USDA's FoodAPS for 2012-2013. We employ propensity score matching to address biases arising from self-selection into WIC. The Healthy Eating Index is used to measure dietary quality and captures the nutritional adequacy of household food purchases relative to dietary guidance. This study aims to provide new insights into program effectiveness along the lines of “nutrition security,” loosely defined as having both an adequate amount of food and quality of food. The findings are expected to provide evidence on how WIC affects food insecurity and dietary outcomes among low-income households, carrying critical implications for policymakers seeking to enhance the design and implementation of nutrition assistance programs.

Md Al Amin and Travis Alan Smith. “The Effects of WIC on Household Food Security and the Quality of Food Acquisitions.” Working paper, University of Georgia.

Dissertation Research

How Would Changes to Community Eligibility Provision Thresholds Affect Child Diet Quality?

Md Al Amin and Travis Alan Smith. Dissertation Chapter 2 / AAEA 2026 accepted conference paper.

Abstract

The Community Eligibility Provision (CEP) allows eligible schools to provide universal free school meals, increasing access to nutritious food for children in high-poverty communities. In 2023, the U.S. Department of Agriculture lowered the minimum Identified Student Percentage (ISP) required for CEP eligibility from 40% to 25%, expanding the number of schools eligible to participate. This study examines how alternative CEP eligibility thresholds would affect child diet quality. We construct a novel school-level panel of CEP eligibility and participation data from academic years 2014–2015 through 2023–2024 and link these data to school demographic characteristics from the National Center for Education Statistics (NCES). To estimate dietary impacts, we combine these data with dietary intake information from the National Health and Nutrition Examination Survey (NHANES) covering 2013–2023. Following prior research, we estimate individual fixed-effects models that relate Healthy Eating Index (HEI-2020) scores to the share of calories consumed from school meals and away-from-home food sources, accounting for unobserved child-specific characteristics. We then simulate changes in diet quality under alternative CEP eligibility thresholds by adjusting school meal consumption among students attending schools that would gain or lose CEP eligibility. Preliminary findings suggest that schools near the eligibility threshold account for a modest share of total enrollment but contain student populations particularly vulnerable to reductions in school meal access. Simulations indicate that stricter CEP eligibility requirements would reduce school meal consumption and lower diet quality, with larger effects among disadvantaged students. These findings provide timely evidence for policymakers evaluating CEP eligibility rules and the role of universal free school meals in promoting child nutrition and reducing dietary disparities.

Md Al Amin and Travis Alan Smith. “How Would Changes to Community Eligibility Provision Thresholds Affect Child Diet Quality?” Working paper, University of Georgia.

Measuring the Distance to Food Sufficiency: A Continuous Approach to Food Hardship

Md Al Amin and Travis Alan Smith. Dissertation Chapter 3.

Abstract

This study examines food hardship using a continuous measure based on self-reported food expenditure shortfalls from the Current Population Survey Food Security Supplement. Conventional food insecurity indicators rely on discrete classifications that mask variation in the severity of deprivation within categories. Following recent work by Craig Gundersen, we use a “resource gap” approach that incorporates the additional dollars households report needing to meet basic food needs . Building on this framework, we construct a normalized needs index by comparing households’ usual food expenditures with their reported shortfalls, allowing for a more interpretable measure of the distance from a food sufficiency threshold. Using CPS Food Security Supplement data from 2002 to 2023, we examine temporal trends and compare this continuous measure with standard food insecurity indicators. Our analysis highlights differences between binary and continuous measures, particularly during periods of economic change, and reveals substantial heterogeneity in hardship within food-insecure populations. We further explore variation across demographic groups and program participation, including SNAP recipients. By extending the use of the resource gap to a longer time horizon and distributional analysis, this study contributes to a more nuanced understanding of food hardship and its policy relevance.

Md Al Amin and Travis Alan Smith. “Measuring the Distance to Food Sufficiency: A Continuous Approach to Food Hardship.” Working paper, University of Georgia.

Does the Informal Economy Undermine Political Stability? Empirical Evidence from Global Perspectives

Accepted, Economics of Governance [Springer Nature] . In production.

Abstract

Political stability (PS) is a cornerstone of economic development, yet the role of the informal economy (IE) in shaping it remains underexplored. This study addresses this gap by examining how the informal and formal economies influence PS across 124 countries from 1996 to 2020, with particular attention to income-group heterogeneity. Using the method of moments quantile regression (MMQR) alongside advanced econometric techniques, the study finds that IE systematically undermines PS while the formal economy (FE) enhances PS that varies meaningfully in magnitude and direction across the PS distribution and income groups. These findings contribute a novel distributional perspective to the literature on economic structure and governance, and carry direct policy implications: reducing informal activity and strengthening institutional frameworks are not interchangeable strategies but context-dependent issue whose effectiveness depends critically on a country's development stage and baseline stability level.

“Does the Informal Economy Undermine Political Stability? Empirical Evidence from Global Perspectives.” Economics of Governance, accepted and in production.

The Role of Fintech and the Shadow Economy in Advancing Sustainable Development Across G7 Countries

Sustainable Development [Wiley] .

Abstract

Achieving sustainable development requires balancing economic growth with environmental conservation, but the relationship between informal economic activities and emerging financial technologies in boosting green growth (GG) is not well understood. The current study identifies this gap by considering the impact of the shadow economy (SE) and financial technology named fintech (FT) on GG in G-7 countries from 1996 to 2020. In contrast to previous studies where these factors are considered separately, the research presents a comprehensive approach that simultaneously investigates the effects of SE and FT across the conditional distribution of GG, a more detailed view of the heterogeneous effects of these two factors. The study uses method of moments quantile regression (MMQR) with fully modified ordinary least squares (FMOLS), dynamic ordinary least squares (DOLS), and Canonical Cointegration Regression (CCR) to confirm robustness. The results indicate that SE has a statistically significant negative impact on both FT and GG as a structural hindrance to sustainable development. Conversely, FT has a positive and significant contribution to GG, which underscores its possible role in promoting environmental sustainability. These findings have robust policy implications: reducing SE and promoting FT ecosystems are two complementary measures needed to promote GG in developed economies.

Relationship between Body Mass Index, Eating Disorders, Internet Addiction, and Social Media Addictions among Adolescents in Bangladesh

Public Health Challenges [Wiley].

Abstract

Background. Due to the increasing consumption of the internet, several outcomes are often seen among adolescents, including an increase in internet addiction (IA) and social media addiction (SMA). It could also have been associated with high body mass index (BMI) and eating disorders (EDs). This study aimed to assess the relationship between BMI, EDs, IA, and SMA among adolescents in Bangladesh and to determine the associated factors.

Methodology. Using a cross-sectional design, this study gathered data from adolescents aged 13–19 years old using an online questionnaire consisting of sociodemographic variables, BMI, eating disorder test scale (EAT-26), Young's Internet Addiction Test scale (IAT-20), and Bergen's Social Media Addiction Scale (BSMAS). Apart from descriptive statistics and Pearson chi-square tests, we employed two sets of binary logistic regression models and a bivariate correlation matrix to analyze the data using SPSS 26.0.

Results. A total of 2,147 adolescents with a mean age of 15.6 years participated in the study, of whom 70.7% were female. We found that 23.2% of participants had eating disorders, 30.8% were addicted to the internet, and 59.9% were addicted to social media. Regarding BMI, 6.6% were underweight, 1.9% were overweight, 24.0% were obese, and 67.5% had normal BMI. Gender, purpose of internet use, daily internet use, physical exercise, reading novels/stories, eating disorders, and BMI were significant predictors of internet addiction and social media addiction. Internet addiction, social media addiction, and eating disorders were positively correlated.

Conclusion. The findings suggest that policymakers, educators, and parents should encourage adolescents to engage in physical exercise and reading habits to reduce internet and social media addiction. Greater parental awareness regarding BMI and eating disorders is also important for improving adolescent health outcomes.

Protected Vegetable Crop Production for Long-term Sustainable Food Security

Journal of Scientific Research and Reports, 30(5), 660–669.

Abstract

A protected culture strategy that offers a fully regulated environment decreases a number of biotic and abiotic stressors. Thinking about protected farming is necessary given the rising food need. The most effective way for meeting the goals of protected horticulture is the greenhouse. The cultivation of many greenhouses has become an important Indian agricultural policy. As a result of the greater productivity levels, these technologies are not only opening doors for producers with larger landholdings, but also for those with smaller holdings. Protected agriculture has provided a new way to produce more in a small space as a result of land holding restrictions, fast urbanisation, falling crop yield, declining biodiversity, and an ever-increasing population. Demand for food, particularly vegetables, has increased significantly as a result of these factors.

Perilous Resurgence of Dengue Fever in Bangladesh: Gender-Based Perspectives on Risk Perception and Adaptation Strategies

Universal Journal of Public Health, 11(5), 751–760.

Abstract

Despite achieving tremendous success in controlling infectious diseases in recent years, Bangladesh has experienced an unprecedented resurgence of dengue fever, necessitating the country to reassess the potential risks and reshape the relevant abatement interventions. This paper examines whether there is any difference in how men and women perceive the risks associated with dengue fever and whether they respond to it differently. The efficacy of mitigation strategies largely depends on the level of compliance, which further requires proper recognition of risks and conformity with related adaptation prescriptions. Gender is deemed a critical factor in understanding the severity of a public health problem and in pursuing appropriate preventive measures. Using cross-sectional survey data collected randomly from urban areas of the Barishal division in Bangladesh, this study employed socio-demographic analysis and logistic regressions to evaluate the gendered perspectives of dengue fever risk and coping measures. The findings from the analysis show that the overall risk perception is very low among respondents. In addition, females consider the threat of dengue fever more seriously than their male counterparts do. Furthermore, a similar trend was observed when adaptation strategies were analyzed; female participants also appeared to be more proactive and careful in taking precautionary measures and following preventive guidelines. As a result, the outcome of this study underscores that gendered insights are crucial for realizing the intensity of a public health threat and thus should be included in designing reduction policies to prevent further diffusion of this dangerous malady.

Md. Tanvir Ahmed and Md. Al Amin. 2023. “Perilous Resurgence of Dengue Fever in Bangladesh: Gender-Based Perspectives on Risk Perception and Adaptation Strategies.” Universal Journal of Public Health, 11(5), 751–760.

Beachgoers' Knowledge, Perceptions, and Willingness to Pay for Sustainable Waste Management in Kuakata Sea Beach, Bangladesh

Nature Environment & Pollution Technology, 23(2).

Abstract

With rising public awareness and concern for environmental sustainability, calls for nature friendly marine and beach litter management have grown louder. This study, employing logistic and ordinary least square regressions, explores tourists’ knowledge, perceptions, and willingness to pay (WTP) using data (n = 400) collected from Kuakata Sea Beach, Bangladesh. Results showed that approximately 99% of the respondents recognize the urgency for further development in the waste management system, while 53% are aware of it. Gender is identified as a statistically significant factor impacting beachgoers’ WTP – males are willing to pay more. Besides, visitors with higher incomes demonstrate the willingness to pay more. Additionally, 37% of the respondents think that appropriate information dissemination and raising awareness are critical to confronting this problem, and another 38% recommended proper placement of dustbins on the beach. These outcomes can be very useful in designing any relevant policies for promoting sustainable beach waste management.

Md Al Amin and Md Tanvir Ahmed. 2024. “Beachgoers' Knowledge, Perceptions, and Willingness to Pay for Sustainable Waste Management in Kuakata Sea Beach, Bangladesh.” Nature Environment & Pollution Technology, 23(2).
